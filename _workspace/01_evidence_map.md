# Evidence Map - Teachability-Gate Synthesis

## Scope

Synthesize existing Phase 7 evidence to determine whether its declared fields
and matrix continuity support a concrete promotion decision. This is a
read-only audit and does not add or reinterpret a health-policy mechanism.

## Sources Reviewed

- `README.md`, `docs/roadmap.md`, `docs/design_principles.md`, and `SPEC.md`.
- `docs/playtest-findings-v0.10.45.md` through
  `docs/playtest-findings-v0.10.48.md`.
- The v0.10.45 instructor debrief-use audit output.
- The v0.10.46 Expert clearability, v0.10.47 command-to-effect, and v0.10.48
  strategy-diversity outputs.

## Mechanisms and Institutions

- The synthesis observes evidence about information visibility, player
  response, operational follow-through, outcomes, explanation, completion,
  command traceability, and descriptive strategy variation.
- It does not add an actor, policy, market, service line, or transition
  mechanism.

## Actor Incentives and Information

- Source profiles are deterministic simulated policies using actor-visible
  observations and legal command surfaces.
- Existing histories and debriefs preserve retrospective traceability but do
  not establish human interpretation or learning.

## Assumptions

- Each source artifact retains its own trace shape and declared evidence
  boundary; the synthesis does not invent a generalized schema.
- Supported coverage is inspectability evidence, not causal evidence.
- Endpoint differences and command-family variation are descriptive only.

## Unresolved Questions

- Whether human players or instructors would find the current evidence clear or
  educationally useful.
- Whether a future reviewer, instructor, or domain finding identifies a gap that
  current observations, histories, diagnostics, and debriefs cannot explain.

## Design Implications

- Keep runtime, difficulty, balance, scoring, and debrief behavior unchanged.
- Require a concrete player-facing, instructor-facing, or domain-review gap
  before promoting runtime work.

## Risks

- Cross-artifact continuity cannot establish human learning, strategy value,
  balance, winnability, calibration, or policy validity.
- Treating complete evidence coverage as proof of clarity would create
  educational opacity and premature runtime promotion.
