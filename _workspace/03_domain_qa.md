## Scope Review

The `v0.10.26` slice is a Phase 7 evidence synthesis. It summarizes existing
competitive findings and does not change runtime mechanics, command validation,
stochastic inputs, scenario schemas, replay formats, state hashes, MCP DTOs,
Python wrapper logic, diagnostics logic, action costs, pledge effects,
difficulty values, or balance.

## Domain Findings

- The synthesis preserves the distinction between simulated-agent evidence and
  human-learning evidence.
- The routing is consistent with the project principle of evidence-led slices
  before broader mechanics or framework expansion.
- Teachability is framed through inspectable tradeoffs, debrief discussion, and
  repeated-play comparison rather than a hidden optimal strategy or new score.
- Future runtime changes remain gated on a concrete mechanics finding.

## Caveats

- The underlying evidence remains simulated-agent, reviewer-policy,
  deterministic-policy, and operator-authored evidence.
- The artifact supports development routing and instructor-facing synthesis, not
  classroom effectiveness, empirical calibration, human comprehension, or policy
  validity.
