# First Scenario Brief

**Status:** Phase 3 design artifact  
**Audience:** Contributors, domain reviewers, and future playtest designers

## Scenario Concept

The first scenario is a short regional-market stabilization challenge. The
player leads a fictional nonprofit health system facing access pressure,
commercial payer tension, state policy scrutiny, workforce strain, and a
regional access coalition opportunity.

The scenario should test the project thesis: outcomes emerge from strategic
institutional response, not from direct policy levers or one-score
optimization.

## Player Role

The player is the health system CEO. The player can allocate organizational
resources and make commitments, but cannot directly control insurers, state
officials, labor representatives, coalition partners, patient behavior, or
reported measurements.

## Scenario Duration

The current prototype uses five executive decision points. A later campaign may
map those turns to quarters, but this brief does not assign calendar duration.

## Learning Objectives

The first scenario should help players practice:

- Balancing financial capacity, access, workforce legitimacy, community trust,
  and policy pressure.
- Reasoning from observed information rather than true omniscient state.
- Anticipating payer, regulator, workforce, and coalition responses.
- Distinguishing invalid operations from valid unfavorable outcomes.
- Explaining why realized outcomes may differ from decision quality.

## Included Interactions

The scenario includes the current interaction set:

- Payer-provider rate negotiation.
- State access-policy response.
- Workforce pressure response.
- Regional access coalition opportunity.
- Rival health system capacity competition response.
- Delayed, noisy, and revised access reporting.
- End-of-run causal debrief from committed history.

## Excluded Interactions

The scenario does not yet include:

- Full Medicare or Medicaid strategic behavior.
- Employer or patient-group strategy.
- Service-line portfolio modeling.
- Individual patient simulation.
- Federal legislative process.
- External scenario loading or save files.
- Empirical calibration or policy forecasting.

## Initial Strategic Tensions

The scenario should preserve these tensions:

- Spending can support access, workforce trust, or legitimacy while reducing
  cash flexibility.
- Aggressive bargaining can improve requested rates but worsen public or
  stakeholder response.
- Policy engagement can reduce oversight risk only when paired with credible
  access commitments.
- Workforce offers must be credible enough to affect trust and labor response.
- Coalition participation can improve community legitimacy but requires shared
  commitments.

## Observations and Uncertainty

The player sees reported access, reported quality, policy briefings, and later
access revisions. Reports may differ from true state through explicit resolved
inputs. Prior observations remain committed even when later revisions arrive.

Future scenario work should define which observations are public, player-facing,
actor-specific, delayed, noisy, biased, or revised. It should not expose true
state to the player as ordinary decision information.

## Debrief Hooks

The debrief should support questions such as:

- What did the player know when the decision was made?
- Which actor responses were driven by credible commitments or outside options?
- Which outcomes came from resource choices, strategic response, or measurement
  uncertainty?
- Which tradeoffs favored the health system but harmed access, trust, or social
  welfare?
- Which decision might still be defensible despite an unfavorable realization?

## Candidate End States

The first scenario should support multiple defensible results:

- Access-stabilization path: stronger access and legitimacy at higher cash cost.
- Fiscal-caution path: better cash preservation with weaker stakeholder gains.
- Aggressive-bargaining path: higher pressure and strategic backlash risk.

These are not victory labels. They are contrasting paths for discussion and
future playtesting.

## Readiness Before Runtime Expansion

Before adding another runtime turn or strategic actor, confirm:

- The actor has an actor card.
- The interaction creates a meaningful player decision.
- The actor decision can leave an inspectable rationale.
- The effect can be attributed in committed history.
- The debrief can explain decision quality separately from outcome quality.
- No scenario loader or generalized framework is required for the slice.
