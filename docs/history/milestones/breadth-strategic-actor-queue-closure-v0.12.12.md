# Simulation Breadth and Strategic Actors Queue Closure — v0.12.12

## Decision

The broader simulation breadth and strategic-actors Future item is complete
for the currently evidenced scope. The competitive campaign already contains
multiple service-line and capacity choices, operating and community outcome
proxies, delayed capital projects, payer interactions, rival systems with
different styles, partial information, monitoring, and debrief attribution.

No concrete unexplained strategy, traceability, or learning gap authorizes a
new runtime mechanism. The queue item is removed without claiming that the
game is a full US health-system, patient, public-payer, or equilibrium model.

## Evidence

- v0.11.11 all-tier evidence: 60/60 complete runs and 1,440 committed
  transitions across profiles, seeds, and difficulty tiers.
- The same artifact contains 10 distinct player command trajectories, no
  common first-month action, and varied final cash, access, quality, workforce
  trust, community trust, and market-share tradeoffs.
- v0.11.12 current-code evidence: 9/9 complete 24-month runs with preserved
  observation, history/hash, and debrief continuity.
- v0.12.3 cross-campaign review: 18/18 complete runs, 270 committed
  transitions, and zero structural gaps.
- Source audits in the closure artifact confirm typed capacity/service-line
  fields, operating outcomes, project and payer commands, rival observation
  boundaries, and debrief attribution markers.

## Boundary and limits

The true state includes all health systems, queued effects, market fields,
policy/event metadata, and private rival actions. The player observation is
restricted to the human system's own reported values, permitted market
signals, lagged public actions, monitoring results, and explicit intelligence
gaps. Instructor debrief may reveal unobserved rival rationales after play.

Medicare and Medicaid are bounded payer command/effect interactions, not full
strategic public-payer actors. Access, trust, market share, treated volume,
and unmet demand are game proxies, not individual patient or validated social-
welfare measures. The evidence is deterministic simulated-policy evidence,
not human learning, causal balance, calibration, policy validity, or general
equilibrium evidence.

## Deferred scope

Full US system modeling, individual patient simulation, validated distributional
outcome categories, full Medicare payment reproduction, Medicaid eligibility
rules, national policy lifecycle modeling, generalized actor frameworks,
portfolio optimization, and broad scenario-authoring infrastructure remain
deferred.

## Reopening condition

Reopen only when new playtest, instructor, scenario, debrief, or domain-review
evidence identifies a concrete unexplained breadth or strategic-actor gap that
the current bounded structures cannot represent.
